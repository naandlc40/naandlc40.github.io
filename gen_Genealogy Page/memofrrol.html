Memo From Rolly


Many thanks to my uncle Eugene, who lived in Quebec City. After my father' s funeral, in 1964, while I was driving him back to Montreal, he told me a few things about the Anglehart's past history and instilled in me an interest in our past. Since then I met many relatives from east to west that are very interested.

After many telephone conversations, purchasing books, reading and many interesting hours, I now I feel happy to present what I presently have accomplished so far.

During that period (1700' s) in Europe the population belonged to rulers. such as Counts who would by killings, beheading and robbery take over your possessions and that was it

To make the journey, it was expensive, or someone hired himself as a laborer so to get across.
 

Usually all males. They were at the mercy of the winds and the sea and sometimes it would take
months. 20 % of the tough sailors died though sickness, diseases, so they would endure a few crossings (only). There were no sanitation aboard and the boats were not so big. No motors.. Calculate what they had to endure!!!

Once across, where were the women? A probable good choice were Indians and Joseph's (Joe ?) wife's name, Marie Annelkawine, sounds quite Indian now. .Joseph must have been born around 1735, for his son was born in 1756. The chance that his son Jean Baptiste, (a French given name} was born in Germany, came back to Canada is close to impossible. Considering surviving three such trips, rough seas, sickness, sanitary conditions, etc. etc.

Alsace-Loraine, where the castle was is now in France, but the population is 80% German.

The only thing that I have a hard time with now, is Nabor, my great grandfather, whom I heard my mother talk about many times. His father was married in St André de Kamouraska, and him in Miguasha. P.Q. ( I do not have those registers and would like to get one. ), but all his children were registered in Paspebiac. To me, he was buried there or St Godefroi.

The end.

Janvier le 15,1997

Il y a plusieurs Anglehart au Canada, éparpillés partout. Englehart anglais à Moncton.
Dans la liste de E-mail il y a 133 Anglehart d’enrégistrés dans le bulletin téléphonique, sans compter la Saskatchewan et l’Alberta qui ne sont pas encore enrégistrés .  Nous sommes tous du même arbre, mais pas nécéssairement de la même branche.

J’ai déjà vu un arbre de famille que quelqu’un avait fait faire par une compagnie généalogique. Ils ont arrêté à certaine place, d’abord pas au complet!

Après plus d’une quinzaine d’années, plusieurs appels téléphoniques, livres d’archive, voyages à bien des locations et des centaines d’heures, finalement je me voilà rendu à ce point ci. J’ai eu bien des peines, car nous sommes 40 ans trop tard. Un gros regret, Ca m’a pris beaucoup  de temps. Je suis heureux qu’il y en a plusieurs qui sont bien intéressés partout, car à mon age, je n’y pensais pas. C’est mon oncle Eugène qui m’a réveillé après le décès de mon père. Nous ne vivions pas dans la même ville, ce qui n’a pas aidé.
Dommage qu’on avait pas les ordinateurs et la technologie que nous possedons maintenant….
Maintenant avec plaisir, j’envois une quarantaine de copies partout. Au moins, il y a plusieurs informantions sur la parentée.
 
My uncle Rolly has asked me to help him and time permitting I don`t mind doing this at all. Now if I can just get others involved. This winter when things are more quiet I plan on doing just that.
