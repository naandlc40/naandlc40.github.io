The Stories from uncle Rolly

Joseph Anglehart: married Marie Annelkawine Place and date unknown. (Her name sounds to be of Canadian Indian origin.)
Marriage certificate states that he was German and the name was written Migkelharte. It was said that two brothers came across, one in Canada and one in the United States. There are some prominent Engelhardt in the USA One at the museum in Buffalo.
I, Rolly, question the above names or spelling! I have a copy of the 1871 census of Bonaventure, Sub district of New Richmond. 71 names that read the Anglehardt, Angleheart, Engleheart. It was common to write names in different spellings. The same for the Irish name, Casey, written Quessey, Caissie.
The purpose of this section is only meant to trace our name Anglehart to Engelhardt and not Migkelharte or Mikel Horth.
January 11th, 1997, I had a meeting with a German Industrialist, a Doctor in Economics who retired here and was involved in Schulteiss brewery in Berlin, who purchased Engelhardt brewery in 1970’s. It does not operate anymore. He knew all the principle owners. In 1920, it was owned by a Jewish group. Later sold to Schulteiss Brewery, which is now owned by a conglomerate of companies.
He says that Annelkawine, Migkelharte and Mikel Horth are not German names.
My grandfather, Eugene, and many other relatives claim that our ancestors did own the Engelhardt Brauerei, in Charlottenberg district in Berlin Germany, and that they also owned a chateau in Alsace- Loraine, now in France, where 80% of the population are Germans. In 1913, I was told by my father and my uncle that a woman came from Germany to St. Godefroi. PQ to have the family sign off the Berlin property for a financial settlement. Supposedly, some relatives were very excited about it. Then the war broke out shortly after, and no one heard anymore. Subsequently John-Hall Kelly, ( a prominent Quebec lawyer ) who was Eugene ( my grandfather ) Anglehart’s cousin, was to look into the affairs while he was a Canadian Ambassador in Ireland, but died at an early age. In 1974, I, Rolly, and my wife Helen, were given a tour of the brewery, in Charlottenberg area of Berlin, and brought back many souvenirs.

Rolly Anglehart

From additional research though I found the our ancestor Joseph Anglehart may have spelled the name differently and most likely Englehardt or Anglehardt.
His wife though was Marie Annel Kawine.

This family was from the Alsace Lorraine area and they spoke German and French. So settling in the Gaspe area was a logical choice. They were allotted a homestead which it appears some of the family is still on. All they had to do then was to clear so much land every year for so many years and that land was yours to keep.

Both father and son had previous military experience as well. At that time Canada was encouraging such people to settle here that had military experience which could be useful if a war with the US did happen as it was anticipated. It actually did happen in 1812 but that was too late to affect them.
